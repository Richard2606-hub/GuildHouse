# ScamShield Typology Database
Version: 2026.07
Source: Regional Consumer Protection Advisories

## TYP-001: Parcel Delivery Scam (Phishing)
Target: Broad consumer base.
Mechanics: 
- Victim receives SMS or WhatsApp claiming a package could not be delivered due to "unpaid customs fee", "missing address info", or "wrong zip code".
- Message contains a link that looks vaguely official but uses a strange top-level domain (e.g., .cc, .top, .vip) or URL shortener.
- Link leads to a fake courier site (PosLaju, J&T, NinjaVan) demanding a small payment (RM 1.00 - RM 5.00) via credit card.
- True goal is credit card detail harvesting.

Red Flags:
- Urgency ("act within 24 hours").
- Generic greeting ("Dear Customer").
- URL mismatch (link text doesn't match actual destination).

## TYP-002: Fake Lottery / Prize Scam
Target: Elderly, low-income groups.
Mechanics:
- Message claims victim won a large cash prize (e.g., RM10,000, RM50,000) or a new car/smartphone from a known brand (Shopee, Lazada, Shell).
- Requires victim to contact a "manager" or click a link to claim the prize.
- Perpetrators will ask for "processing fees", "taxes", or "insurance" to release the funds.

Red Flags:
- You cannot win a lottery you did not enter.
- Legitimate sweepstakes do not ask winners to pay upfront fees.

## TYP-003: Impersonation of Authorities (LHDN / Police / Bank Negara)
Target: High-income individuals, professionals.
Mechanics:
- Caller/message claims victim's identity was used in money laundering, tax evasion, or drug trafficking.
- Threatens immediate arrest or account freezing.
- Demands funds be transferred to a "safe account" (usually an individual's personal account) for auditing.

Red Flags:
- Government agencies will never ask you to transfer money to a personal "safe account".
- Use of fear and extreme urgency.
